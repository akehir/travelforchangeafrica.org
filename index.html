Volunteering

No matter your background, your interest, or your experience - our tailor-made volunteering programs ensure you can fully leverage your skills to have the biggest impact - both for you and the community you are working in.

Our volunteering programs are:

  • Tailored! Tell us your skills, interest, ideas, and we will design a programme for you
  • Flexible in Duration! we offer volunteering opportunities tailored to your schedule, from a few days up to several months
  • Cheap! We have a lot of experience on the ground and know where to cut unnecessary cost while maintaining all the quality
  • High-Impact! Our experience combined with our local organisations ensure that you actually have an impact and can improve lives
  • Authentic! You are emerging in the local environment, become part of the community, make friends for life and can affect, inspire, and change many lives

If you are interested in volunteering, contact us for more information, and we will design an individually tailored volunteering program together with you!

Volunteer through our local partners

We are partnering with local community-based organizations which are experienced with hosting volunteers and ensure a high impact of your work. Currently, we offer volunteering work through Living Positive Kenya, whose mission it is to alleviate the suffering of women and children infected or otherwise affected by HIV/AIDS.

Depending on the current needs as well as your skills and interests, volunteering assignments at the CBO can include.

  • Teaching & Tutoring
  • Child Care
  • HIV AIDS awareness & education
  • Women’s Empowerment
  • Administrative duties
  • Social Media & Web Design
  • Microfinancing
  • Business Plan developments
  • Farming/ Agriculture
  • Construction
  • Fundraising

Help us grow and volunteer with us

Since we are always striving to improve our organisation’s impact and striving to include more CBO’s across the region, you can also directly volunteer at Travel for Change. Potential tasks here can include.

  • Business Development
  • Program Management
  • Marketing/Communication
  • Fundraising
  • Partnership Development

We have 3 major programs that work together to shape our goal as an organization, which is to

  • To raise HIV-positive women up from their life of rejection and hopelessness (HIV Intervention)
  • Teach them skills and help them start a business (WEEP) and
  • Make sure their children are cared for during the process (OVC)

In partnership with Living Positive, we are also running and funding a feeding and schooling program Orphans and Vulnerable Children (OVC). Children in this program are those infected or affected by HIV/AIDS. They may be living with a sick parent, or orphaned and living with an extended family member.

While a part of your volunteering fee goes to our local CBO’s for administration and business development reasons, part of it will go directly to our OVC Program and will

  • Education: Provision of school fees for OVCs in boarding primary and secondary schools
  • Feeding programme: Provision of daily lunch for 85 OVCs in daycare

WITH 5USD, you can feed a child for one week!

Culture, Nature, Adventure - whatever you would like to know more of your host country. About Travel for Change

Kenya! Lush savannah, rolling plains, spectacular wildlife, and yet, her most alluring attraction is her people; their culture and heritage. At Travel for Change, we facilitate seamless integration of traditional vacationing with community-based tourism. Experience the magic that Kenya has to offer, while you add value to those less privileged, exchange ideas with them, changing not only their lives but yours as well; hence getting to travel for change.

Who are we?

Travel for Change is a non governmental organization that supports and runs community based development and sustenance projects in Kenya, while offering those from more privileged society abroad a chance to interact with Kenya’s diverse cultures in ways that can change the world. We provide a platform for philanthropic tourism.

Who can be involved?

Literally everyone and anyone who wants to make a change in the lives of others can be part of travel for change. If you either want to volunteer while touring or touring while volunteering, we have tailor made packages suited to your preferences. From experienced professionals who want to share their skills and time to add value to peoples lives while exploring Kenya’s white sandy beaches; to intern volunteers who would gain learning experiences from diverse cultures while taking ‘selfies’ in Kenya’s breathtaking sceneries and landscapes; to groups and families who would want to actively interact in projects carried out by the community while watching a pride of lions grooming in the savannah.

Who benefits?

Ours is a mutually symbiotic relationship. Our indigenous communities benefit immensely by gaining much needed support and resources that are life-changing and sustainable, while you get to benefit by sharing in experiences that are bound to change your perspective of our world while appreciating its beauty and grandeur.

Maasai Mara Game Reserve

At the heart of Kenya’s Rift Valley, in the rolling plains and lush savannah, lies the majestic Maasai Mara National Reserve in its entire splendor. Perhaps Africa’s most diverse eco-system, the reserve is home to all the Big Five; Lion, Leopard, African Elephant, African Buffalo and Black Rhinoceros. It also boasts the annual Great Wildebeest Migration, one of the “seven wonders of the world” generally referred to as the “world cup of the wild”. Other animals in attendance include zebra, Thompson’s gazelle and the main antagonists, the Nile crocodile. This breathtaking spectacle occurs every year from July to October as the animals cross the Mara River into neighboring Tanzania’s Serengeti National Park. The Maasai Mara Game Reserve and its environs are home to the Maasai people, one of Africa’s most renowned cultures with preserved heritage.

Amboseli National Park

Famous for being the best place in Africa to see up-close free-ranging elephants, is the Amboseli National Park. Located in Loitoktok, Kajiado County, south west of Nairobi, it offers a breathtaking view of Mt Kilimanjaro in Tanzania, the highest mountain in Africa. At Amboseli National Park you also have the opportunity to meet and interact with the Maasai people in their intricately designed regalia and colourful ornaments. The park is also home to other diverse species including; Tanzanian cheetahs, cape buffalos, Maasai lions, spotted hyenas, Maasai giraffes, plain zebras and blue wildebeest.

Lake Nakuru National Park

Lake Nakuru is the ultimate bird watchers paradise. At the floor of the Great Rift Valley lies this beautiful soda lake at an altitude of 1754m above sea level.   Due to algae profusion, the lake attracts a vast quantity of flamingoes and 400 other bird species. This picturesque landscape is also home to a vast array of different flora and fauna among them; baboon, waterbuck and different plant species including the biggest euphorbia forest in Africa. The park is also home to the white rhino, a species that was recently at the brink of extinction. The towering escarpments and ridges in the vicinity of the park also offer great scenic beauty.

Samburu National Reserve

Centre-stage in the best-selling book “Born Free” by George and Joy Adamson, about Elsa the Lioness- an orphaned cub raised by the authors- is the Samburu National Reserve. This semi-arid and remote reserve, located 350km from Nairobi, derives its name from the Samburu people; closely related but distinct from the famous Maasai. The Samburu National Reserve is renowned for its rare species of animals unique to the park, namely; unique Grevy’s Zebra, reticulated giraffe, Besia onyx, greater and lesser kudu, gerenuk, Somali ostrich, and pancake tortoise. Other animals include cheetahs, lions, elephants, buffalo, hippos and the elusive Kenya leopard. It is also a bird haven with more than 350 avian species including vultures, marabous, and Somali ostriches.

Aberdare National Park

Strikingly amazing scenery comprising of indigenous forest, rugged terrain, deep ravines, open moorlands, streams and waterfalls characterize the great scenic beauty that is the Aberdare National Park. Located 100km north of Nairobi, the park hosts animals such as the leopard, African elephant, African hunting dog, giant forest hog, bushbuck, mountain reedbuck, waterbuck, cape buffalo, side-striped jackal, eland, olive baboon, black and white colobus monkey, and sykes monkey. Rarer sightings include those of the golden cat, African wild cat, the blue duiker and the bongo. The park also offers a rewarding bird-viewing experience. Trout fishing, picnicking and camping in the moorland are favorite pastimes in the park.

Lake Naivasha, Crescent Island

One of Kenya’s best kept secrets is Cresent Island Park, an island on Lake Naivasha. This getaway found in the Great Rift Valley of Kenya, 2 hours away from Nairobi is considered one of the most beautiful parks in Kenya. The park boasts more animals per acre than any other park in Kenya. This island gem is private, secure and patrolled by Guides who can accompany anyone who wishes for a guided walk. The shores host abundant birdlife especially huge numbers of pelicans, cormorants and fish-eagles with their haunting cry. Herds of wildebeest, zebra, waterbuck and gazelles roam the terrain. Other animals to be sighted are hippos, giraffes and buffaloes among others.

Tsavo West National Park

Tsavo West National Park is located in the Coast province of Kenya, The A109 road Nairobi-Mombasa and a railway divides it from the adjoining Tsavo East National Park. From the sight of fifty million gallons of crystal clear water gushing out of parched lava rock that is the Mzima Springs, to the Shetani lava flows, Tsavo West is a beautiful, rugged wilderness. The savannah ecosystem comprises of open grasslands, scrublands, and Acacia woodlands, belts of riverine vegetation and rocky ridges. From the Poacher's Lookout visitors can see the teeming herds of game in the plains below. Tsavo West offers some of the most magnificent game viewing in the world and attractions include elephant, rhino, Hippos, lions, cheetah, leopards and buffalos. The park is habitat to diverse plant and bird species including the threatened corncrake and near threatened Basra Reed Warbler.

Lake Manyara National Park

Heralded by Nobel laureate Ernest Hemingway as “the loveliest I have seen”, Lake Manyara is truly just that; the loveliest of gems. Lake Manyara National Park is located 126 km (78 mi) south west of Arusha in Tanzania. More than 400 species of birds inhabit the park offering a good spot for bird watching. African leopards, Masai lions, Tanzanian cheetahs, African bush elephants, blue monkeys, dik-dik, Thomson's gazelle, hippo, Masai giraffe, impala, Grant's zebras and more inhabit the park and many can be seen throughout the year.

Tarangire National Park

Tarangire National Park is a surprise package in the Tanzania’s northern tourism circuit. Located near the all famous Serengeti National Park and Ngororoni Crater, this little gem is often overlooked by many visitors. The park usually has a huge concentration of animals during peak seasons with only a fraction of the tourists found in other parks. The name of the park originates from the Tarangire River that crosses through the park. Being the only source of water for wild animals during dry seasons there is an influx of game from other parks such as the Manyara National Park during this period; usually from July through to October. The park is ideal for those seeking a quiet getaway away from the bustle of other visitors as is normally in other parks. Tarangire National Park is famous for its huge number of elephants, baobab trees and tree climbing lions. Home to more than 550 bird species, the park is a haven for bird enthusiasts who can expect to see dozens of species even in the dry season. In 2015, a giraffe that is white due to leucism was spotted in the park. Other animals inhabiting this park include zebra, wildebeest, olive baboons and waterbuck.

Ngorongoro Crater

As the world’s largest intact volcanic caldera, the Ngorongoro crater is one of the most beautiful natural wildlife safari sites in Africa and an ideal location to interact with local Maasai tribe. It is located 180 km west of Arusha Town in the Crater Highlands area of Tanzania. The crater has one of the densest known populations of Masaai lions and home to the highest density of big game in Africa which include the Big Five. The Ngorongoro Conservation Area also protects Olduvai Gorge, a site considered to be the seat of humanity after the discovery of the earliest known specimens of the human genus, Homo habilis as well as early hominidae, such as Paranthropus boisei. Volcanic craters in this region form some of the most fertile grazing grounds in Africa. A healthy population of black rhino, buffalos, hippos and some of the largest tusker elephants left in Africa inhabit the area. There also are many other ungulates: the blue wildebeest, Grant's zebra, common eland, grants and Thompson gazelles.

Serengeti National Park

As Tanzania’s oldest and most popular park, Serengeti National Park is as scintillating as they come. The park is famous for its annual migration of over 1.5 million white bearded wildebeest. 250,000 zebra and 300,000 gazelles join the wildebeests across murky crocodile infested rivers in search of greener pastures. This magnificent feature has earned the company world heritage status. The park lies in northwestern Tanzania, to the north which lies the Kenyan border, where it is continuous with the Maasai Mara National Reserve. To the southeast of the park is the Ngorongoro Conservation Area, to the southwest lies Maswa Game Reserve, to the west are the Ikorongo and Grumeti Game Reserves, and to the northeast and east lies the Loliondo Game Control Area. Together, these areas form the larger Serengeti ecosystem. The park is usually described as being divided into three regions;
Serengeti plains: the almost treeless grassland of the south is the most emblematic scenery of the park. This is where the wildebeest breed, as they remain in the plains from December to May. Other hoofed animals - zebra, gazelle, impala, hartebeest, topi, buffalo, and waterbuck - also occur in huge numbers during the wet season. "Kopjes" are granite florations that are very common in the region, and they are great observation posts for predators, as well as a refuge for hyrax and pythons.
Western corridor: the black clay soil covers the swampy savannah of this region. The Grumeti River is home to Nile crocodiles, colobus monkeys, hippopotamus, and martial eagles. The migration passes through from May to July.
Northern Serengeti: the landscape is dominated by open woodlands (predominantly Commiphora) and hills, ranging from Seronera in the south to the Mara River on the Kenyan border. Apart from the migratory wildebeest and zebra (which occur from July to August, and in November), the bushy savannah is the best place to find elephant, giraffe, and dik dik.
